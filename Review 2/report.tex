\section{Problem Formulation \& Targeted Scope}

\textbf{Application Domain: Smart Energy Grids (Short-Term Load Forecasting)}

Reliable short-term load forecasting (STLF) is essential for modern smart grid operations, enabling efficient demand-side management and grid stability. However, residential energy data is highly sensitive and subject to privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR, IEEE 2030.5). Traditional centralized learning models risk privacy leakage and fail to account for the \textbf{non-IID (Independent and Identically Distributed)} nature of energy consumption across diverse households. Residential loads exhibit extreme heterogeneity due to varying appliances (e.g., EV chargers vs. basic lighting) and occupancy patterns. Furthermore, \textbf{temporal drift} (seasonal changes, holidays, or appliance upgrades) causes static models to degrade rapidly.

\textbf{Problem Statement:}
To design and implement a decentralized, \textbf{Federated Personalized Drift-Aware Attention Framework (FPDAF)} for multivariate energy forecasting. The system aims to:
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep, leftmargin=1.5em]
\item Address client heterogeneity through localized personalization layers ($FedPer$ architecture).
\item Detect and mitigate localized concept drift using the \textbf{Cumulative Sum (CUSUM)} algorithm.
\item Provide interpretable insights via \textbf{Temporal Attention Mechanisms} for grid operators to trust forecasts.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Targeted Dataset:} \textbf{Pecan Street Public Dataset}, which contains research-grade multivariate electricity data (voltage, power, weather) from 100+ residential sites at 1-minute to 15-minute granularity.

\section{Research Novelty \& Technical Framework}

\textbf{Adaptive Drift Monitoring (CUSUM integration):}
The core innovation of FPDAF is the integration of online statistical monitoring within the federated loop. Instead of periodic retraining, we monitor the local loss residual $e_t = |y_t - \hat{y}_t|$.
The CUSUM statistic $S_t$ is updated locally:
\[
S_t = \max(0, S_{t-1} + (e_t - \mu - k))
\]
where $\mu$ is the target mean, $k$ is the slack parameter (typically $0.5\sigma$), and $h$ is the decision threshold. If $S_t > h$, a \textbf{Drift Alert} triggers an isolated local update of the personalization head, preventing drifted gradients from entering the global aggregation.

\textbf{Explainable Attention Mechanism:}
To provide transparency, we integrate an attention layer over the LSTM/Transformer backbone. This allows the model to assign weights $\alpha_i$ to past signal windows $h_i$:
\[
e_i = v^\top \tanh(W_h h_i + W_x x_t + b), \quad \alpha_i = \frac{\exp(e_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^{T} \exp(e_j)}
\]
High $\alpha_i$ values highlight critical past consumption periods (e.g., a similar demand spike 24 hours ago), allowing operators to verify the model's reliance on historical periodicity.

\textbf{Privacy and Differential Privacy ($\epsilon$-DP):}
The framework ensures privacy via two layers:
1. \textbf{Structural Privacy:} raw meter data never leaves the local smart gateway; only model updates are exchanged.
2. \textbf{Differential Privacy:} To prevent \textbf{gradient inversion attacks}, we inject calibrated Laplacian noise into the gradients before transmission. The noise scale is determined by the sensitivity $\Delta f$ and the privacy budget $\epsilon$: $Lap(\Delta f / \epsilon)$. This ensures no individual household signature can be reconstructed from the aggregated global weights.
