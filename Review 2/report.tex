\section{Project Proposal and objectives}
\textbf{Overall Project Idea:} Instead of merely implementing standard off-the-shelf algorithms, this project proposes a novel mathematical extension to existing FedAvg and FedPer models. We introduce the fully decentralized \textbf{Federated Personalized Drift-Aware Attention Framework (FPDAF)} specifically designed for multivariate healthcare time-series analysis (e.g., ICU risk forecasting).

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Primary Novelty \& Algorithmic Innovation:} 
The definitive novelty of FPDAF lies in its dynamic hybrid integration. Unlike conventional FL architectures that maintain blind static global averaging, our extension mathematically intercepts the \textit{FedAvg} loop. We inject a \textbf{CUSUM Statistical Trigger} directly into local active hospital nodes, enabling isolated Neural Network layers (Personalization Heads) to mathematically detach and adapt in real-time when patient physiology rapidly deteriorates, utilizing \textbf{Temporal Attention} to explain exactly which vitals caused the deviation.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Objectives to Achieve:}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep, leftmargin=1.5em]
    \item \textbf{Privacy-Preserving Decentralized Training:} Implement a multi-hospital training cycle using PyTorch that ensures individual patient records never leave the local client node.
    \item \textbf{Mitigate Client Heterogeneity:} Resolve the global averaging mismatch by implementing \textbf{Local Personalization Heads} (\textit{FedPer}) for site-specific clinical fine-tuning.
    \item \textbf{Active Temporal Drift Detection:} Embed a mathematical \textbf{CUSUM Drift Tracker} to monitor prediction confidence and trigger automated local weight resets upon patient physiological status shift.
    \item \textbf{Clinician-Centric Explainability:} Deploy \textbf{Temporal Attention Mechanisms} to highlight precisely which ICU vital signs (e.g., Blood Pressure drop + Heart Rate spike) contributed to the risk prediction.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Introduction and Background}
Continuous monitoring systems in Intensive Care Units (ICUs) collect immense streams of physiological data (HR, BP, SpO2). Early detection of patient deterioration using Deep Learning models can prevent fatalities like Sepsis or Cardiac Arrest. However, massive clinical models require centralized data, violating privacy laws. While Federated Learning (e.g., FedAvg) resolves the data-sharing issue, it fails to account for non-IID data distributions (how demographics and hospital protocols vary significantly between facilities).

Our literature review explored standard Federated methods. FedAvg (McMahan 2017) trains efficiently but lacks personalization. FedPer (Arivazhagan 2020) personalizes models to local distributions but lacks any mathematical mechanism for tracking concept drift over time. FedXAI works (Kalakoti 2025) focus mostly on classification datasets, not complex high-frequency critical care time-series. Thus, a significant gap exists in achieving privacy, personalization, drift monitoring, and clinical explainability simultaneously.

\section{Architecture diagram and explanation of the design}
The FPDAF architecture is a split-layer design. The \textbf{Global Temporal Backbone} consists of the LSTM layers responsible for learning generalized physiological laws across all participating hospitals.

The \textbf{Attention Layer} sits atop the backbone to compute temporal context vectors based on recent physiological spikes, giving transparent $x_i$ timestamps feature importance. Finally, the \textbf{Personalization Head} is isolated completely to the hospital node; it never transfers its gradients to the server, ensuring the final clinical decision is strictly correlated to the local hospital's demographic distribution.

\section{Scientific Methodology \& Neural Formalism}
The innovation of FPDAF is formalized as a split-weight optimization problem across $K$ hospital nodes. The local predictive output $\hat{y}_t^k$ is defined by:
\[ \hat{y}_t^k = h_{\phi^k} \left( g_{\theta} (X_{1:t}^k) \cdot \alpha_t \right) \] 
where $g_{\theta}$ is the \textbf{Global Temporal Backbone} (synchronized via FedAvg), $h_{\phi^k}$ is the \textbf{Private Personalization Head} (site-specific), and $\alpha_t$ represents the \textbf{Temporal Attention context vector}. 

\vspace{0.2cm}
To handle temporal drift, we implement the \textbf{CUSUM Neural Trigger}. The system monitors the cumulative deviation of local prediction residuals:
\[ S_t = \max(0, S_{t-1} + |y_t - \hat{y}_t| - \mu - k) \]
If $S_t > H$ (the statistical threshold), the framework automatically declares a patient state-drift, triggering a local backpropagation cycle on the personalization head $\phi^k$ while keeping $\theta$ frozen, preserving the global model's generalizability while adapting to the patient's acute deterioration.
