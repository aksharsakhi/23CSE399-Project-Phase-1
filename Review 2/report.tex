\section{Project Proposal and objectives}
\textbf{Overall Project Goal:} Instead of merely implementing standard off-the-shelf algorithms, this project \textbf{sets the objective to develop} a novel extension to existing FedAvg and FedPer models. We will introduce the fully decentralized \textbf{Federated Personalized Drift-Aware Attention Framework (FPDAF)} specifically designed for multivariate healthcare time-series analysis (e.g., ICU risk forecasting).

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Primary Novelty \& Proposed Algorithmic Innovation:} 
The definitive novelty of FPDAF lies in its planned hybrid integration. Unlike conventional FL architectures that maintain blind static global averaging, our extension \textbf{proposes to intercept} the \textit{FedAvg} loop. We will inject a \textbf{Novel CUSUM Extension} directly into local active hospital nodes, enabling isolated Neural Network layers to act as an \textbf{Automated Neural Trigger}. This allows the model to "unfreeze" and adapt in real-time when patient physiology rapidly deteriorates, utilizing \textbf{Temporal Attention} to explain exactly which vitals caused the deviation.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Key Research Pillars:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep, leftmargin=1.5em]
    \item \textbf{Novel FedAvg Extension:} Uses \textbf{Attention-Aware Weighting} to prioritize higher-confidence hospital data during the global update instead of just simple patient-count averages.
    \item \textbf{Novel CUSUM Extension:} Repurposes statistical monitoring as an active neural manipulator for instant clinical adaptation.
    \item \textbf{Novel Local Personalization:} Extends standard \textit{FedPer} nodes to focus on specific hospital demographics without losing shared global intelligence.
\end{itemize}

\section{Introduction and Background}
\textbf{Clinical Context \& Terminology:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep, leftmargin=1.5em]
    \item \textbf{Sepsis:} Life-threatening organ failure caused by infection; needs early detection.
    \item \textbf{Deterioration:} Rapid worsening of patient condition (vitals becoming abnormal).
    \item \textbf{Profiling:} Analyzing time-series data (Heart Rate, BP) to predict patient risk.
\end{itemize}

Continuous monitoring systems in Intensive Care Units (ICUs) collect immense streams of physiological data (HR, BP, SpO2). Early detection of patient deterioration using Deep Learning models can prevent fatalities. However, massive clinical models require centralized data, violating privacy laws. While Federated Learning (e.g., FedAvg) resolves the data-sharing issue, it often fails to account for hospital-specific demographics and protocols (Accuracy Gap) or lacks clinician trust (Trust Gap).

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) Mapping:}

    \item \textbf{Primary (SDG 3):} Directly contributes to Good Health by enabling explainable patient risk prediction.
    \item \textbf{Secondary (SDG 9 \& 16):} Supports scalable clinical innovation and ethical data privacy security.
\end{itemize}

\section{Architecture diagram and explanation of the design}
The FPDAF architecture is a split-layer design as shown in Figure 1. It utilizes a centralized server for global weight aggregation while maintaining strictly isolated local personalization heads at each hospital node to ensure privacy and specialized clinical accuracy.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering

The \textbf{Attention Layer} sits atop the backbone to compute temporal context vectors based on recent physiological spikes, giving transparent $x_i$ timestamps feature importance. Finally, the \textbf{Personalization Head} is isolated completely to the hospital node; it never transfers its gradients to the server, ensuring the final clinical decision is strictly correlated to the local hospital's demographic distribution.

\section{Scientific Methodology \& Neural Formalism}
The innovation of FPDAF is formalized as a split-weight optimization problem across $K$ hospital nodes. The local predictive output $\hat{y}_t^k$ is defined by:
\[ \hat{y}_t^k = h_{\phi^k} \left( g_{\theta} (X_{1:t}^k) \cdot \alpha_t \right) \] 
where $g_{\theta}$ is the \textbf{Global Temporal Backbone} (synchronized via FedAvg), $h_{\phi^k}$ is the \textbf{Private Personalization Head} (site-specific), and $\alpha_t$ represents the \textbf{Temporal Attention context vector}. 

\vspace{0.2cm}
To handle temporal drift, we implement the \textbf{CUSUM Neural Trigger}. The system monitors the cumulative deviation of local prediction residuals:
\[ S_t = \max(0, S_{t-1} + |y_t - \hat{y}_t| - \mu - k) \]
If $S_t > H$ (the statistical threshold), the framework automatically declares a patient state-drift, triggering a local backpropagation cycle on the personalization head $\phi^k$ while keeping $\theta$ frozen, preserving the global model's generalizability while adapting to the patient's acute deterioration.
